<p><p>The bilirubin meter market comprises two main types: bench-top and transcutaneous devices. Bench-top bilirubin meters are laboratory instruments typically used for precise, high-volume testing of bilirubin levels in newborns and other patients. They offer accurate results and are commonly found in hospitals. Conversely, transcutaneous bilirubin meters are portable, non-invasive devices that measure bilirubin levels through the skin, making them ideal for quick assessments in outpatient settings and reducing the need for blood draws. Both types cater to different clinical needs.</p></p>
