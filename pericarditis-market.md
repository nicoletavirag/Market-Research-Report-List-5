<p><ul><li>Acute Pericarditis</li><li>Chronic Pericarditis</li><li>Recurrent Pericarditis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Acute pericarditis is characterized by sudden inflammation of the pericardium, causing chest pain and possible complications such as pericardial effusion. Chronic pericarditis involves persistent inflammation over a longer period of time, leading to thickening and scarring of the pericardium. Recurrent pericarditis refers to multiple episodes of inflammation, often occurring after an initial episode of acute pericarditis. Understanding the different types of pericarditis is crucial for accurate diagnosis and appropriate management of the condition.</p></p>
