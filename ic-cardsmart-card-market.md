<p><p>The IC Card Smart Card market is divided into two primary types: contact and contactless. Contact smart cards require physical insertion into a reader, facilitating secure data exchange through direct electrical contact. This method is commonly used in scenarios requiring high security, such as banking and government identification. In contrast, contactless smart cards enable data transmission via radio-frequency identification (RFID), allowing communication without physical contact. This technology is ideal for quick transactions, such as public transportation and access control systems.</p></p>
