<p><p>Structured finance encompasses various market types, including Asset Backed Securities (ABS), Collateralized Debt Obligations (CDO), and Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS). ABS involves pooling various assets as collateral for securities, while CDOs repackage debt into tranches with varying levels of risk and return. MBS represent claims to the cash flows from pools of mortgages. These markets allow for the efficient transfer of risk and help investors diversify their portfolios by providing exposure to different types of underlying assets.</p></p>
