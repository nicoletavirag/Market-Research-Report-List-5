<p><p>The Human Microbiome Therapeutics market comprises two primary types: Fecal Microbiota Transplantation (FMT) and microbiome drugs. FMT involves transferring stool from a healthy donor to restore a patient’s microbiome, often used for conditions like recurrent Clostridium difficile infections. Microbiome drugs, on the other hand, are pharmaceutical products designed to modulate the microbiome through specific metabolites or engineered strains to treat various diseases. Both approaches aim to harness the therapeutic potential of gut microbiota in enhancing health outcomes.</p></p>
