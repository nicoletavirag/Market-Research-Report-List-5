<p><strong>The Brain Computer Interface Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-invasive</li><li>Partially invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) market is categorized into three types: invasive, non-invasive, and partially invasive. Invasive BCIs involve surgically implanted devices that directly interact with neuronal tissue, offering high accuracy but carrying surgical risks. Non-invasive BCIs utilize external sensors to capture brain activity without surgery, making them safer but often less precise. Partially invasive BCIs strike a balance, employing implanted devices that do not penetrate brain tissue, providing improved performance while minimizing surgical complications. Each type serves distinct applications in medical and non-medical fields.</p></p>
