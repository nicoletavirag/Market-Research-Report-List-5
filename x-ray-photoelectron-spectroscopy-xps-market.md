<p><p>X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) is an analytical technique used to study the surface chemistry of materials by measuring the elemental composition and chemical state of surfaces. The XPS market can be segmented into monochromatic and non-monochromatic types. Monochromatic XPS systems utilize a single-energy X-ray source, providing higher energy resolution and more precise chemical state information. In contrast, non-monochromatic systems use a broad spectrum of X-rays, offering faster data acquisition but with lower resolution, making them suitable for bulk analysis and quick assessments.</p></p>
