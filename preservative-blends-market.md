<p><p>Preservative blends are categorized into different types based on their chemical composition and mode of action. Parabens are widely used for their effectiveness against a wide range of microorganisms. Formaldehyde-based preservatives work by releasing formaldehyde gas to kill bacteria and fungi. Halogenated preservatives contain chlorine or bromine compounds for antimicrobial properties. Alcohols like ethanol and isopropanol are effective against bacteria and viruses. Organic acids such as benzoic acid and sorbic acid inhibit microbial growth. Other preservatives include natural alternatives like essential oils and plant extracts. Each type of preservative blend has its specific applications and advantages in different industries.</p></p>
