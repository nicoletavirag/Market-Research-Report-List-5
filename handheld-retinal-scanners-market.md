<p><p>Handheld retinal scanners are categorized into two main types based on the pupil dilation requirement: no mydriasis and mydriasis. </p><p>**No Mydriasis** scanners function without the need for pupil dilation, allowing for quick, comfortable, and non-invasive eye examinations. </p><p>**Mydriasis** scanners require the pupil to be dilated to enhance retinal visibility, enabling detailed imaging of the retina. While mydriatic procedures can improve diagnostic accuracy, they may involve discomfort and longer preparation times for patients.</p></p>
