<p><p>The nanofabrication market is categorized into traditional and non-traditional processing methods. Traditional processing includes established techniques like photolithography and etching, which rely on optical methods to create nanoscale patterns on substrates. In contrast, non-traditional processing encompasses newer approaches such as self-assembly, nanoimprint lithography, and fabrication through directed assembly, which leverage innovative principles to achieve nanoscale features. While traditional methods provide precision, non-traditional methods often offer cost-effectiveness and scalability, catering to diverse industry needs in nanotechnology.</p></p>
