<p><ul><li>Microfiltration</li><li>Ultrafiltration</li><li>Nanofiltration</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hollow fiber membrane market consists of three primary types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration employs larger pore sizes to remove suspended solids and bacteria, making it suitable for water purification and food processing. Ultrafiltration has smaller pores, allowing it to filter out viruses and large molecular weight solutes. Nanofiltration, with even finer pores, effectively removes divalent ions and small organic molecules, making it ideal for water softening and wastewater treatment. These techniques enable efficient separation and purification across various applications.</p></p>
