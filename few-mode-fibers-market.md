<p><p>Few-mode fibers are classified based on the number of supported modes, with common categories being 2-mode, 4-mode, and others. 2-mode fibers primarily transmit two distinct light paths, optimizing for simplicity and performance in specific applications. 4-mode fibers support four modes, enhancing data throughput and transmission efficiency, suitable for advanced communication systems. Other modes may include specialized configurations for niche applications, focusing on unique benefits such as increased bandwidth, lower loss, and improved modal dispersion, catering to evolving market needs.</p></p>
