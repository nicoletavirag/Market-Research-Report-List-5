<p><p>The non-fat dry milk market is segmented into low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat types based on the processing temperature and resulting functionality. Low-heat non-fat dry milk, processed at lower temperatures, retains more nutrients and is ideal for making yogurt and infant formula. Medium-heat is suitable for baked goods and dairy blends, offering moderate heat stability. High-heat non-fat dry milk, processed at higher temperatures, enhances shelf life and is primarily used in food products requiring longer shelf stability, like creamers and dry mixes.</p></p>
