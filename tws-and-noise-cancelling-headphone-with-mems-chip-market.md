<p><p>The TWS (True Wireless Stereo) and noise-cancelling headphone market is categorized into two primary types: passive noise reduction and active noise cancellation. Passive noise reduction relies on physical design and materials to block external sounds, offering a simple, effective barrier against noise. In contrast, active noise cancellation employs advanced technologies, including MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) chips, which analyze ambient sounds and generate opposing sound waves, effectively reducing unwanted noise. Both technologies aim to enhance the listening experience in various environments.</p></p>
