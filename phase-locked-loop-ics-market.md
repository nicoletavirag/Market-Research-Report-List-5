<p><p>Phase Locked Loop (PLL) integrated circuits (ICs) are categorized into several types based on their design and application. APLL (Analog PLL) uses analog components for frequency locking, DPLL (Digital PLL) employs digital processing for better stability and noise resistance, ADPLL (All-Digital PLL) is purely digital for enhanced integration and simplicity, SPLL (Switched-capacitor PLL) uses switched-capacitor techniques for precise frequency control, and CPPLL (Charge Pump PLL) utilizes charge pump circuits for improved output stability. Each type serves unique applications across communications and signal processing.</p></p>
