<p><p>The 1,4-Butanediol market features various production processes. The Reppe process synthesizes butanediol through acetylene and formaldehyde, while the Davy process involves reducing maleic anhydride with hydrogen. The Butadiene process utilizes butadiene as a precursor in a multi-step reaction. Lastly, the Propylene oxide process converts propylene oxide into butanediol through hydrolysis. Each method offers distinct advantages and cost structures, influencing market dynamics and supply chain strategies within the butanediol industry.</p></p>
