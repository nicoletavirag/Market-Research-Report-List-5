<p><p>The electrodes used in medical devices are classified into two main types: disposable and reusable electrodes. Disposable medical electrodes are designed for single-use, ensuring hygiene and minimizing the risk of infection, making them ideal for procedures like ECG monitoring. Reusable medical electrodes, on the other hand, are crafted for multiple uses, often made from durable materials that can withstand sterilization processes. They are commonly employed in long-term monitoring applications, balancing cost-effectiveness and functionality in patient care.</p></p>
