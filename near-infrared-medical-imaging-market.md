<p><p>The Near Infrared Medical Imaging market consists of two primary device types: Near Infrared Fluorescence Imaging Devices and Near Infrared Bioluminescence Imaging Devices. Near Infrared Fluorescence Imaging Devices utilize fluorescent probes that emit light when excited, enabling precise visualization of tissues and physiological processes, often aiding in cancer surgery and imaging of vascular structures. Near Infrared Bioluminescence Imaging Devices, on the other hand, involve the use of bioluminescent agents that emit light naturally, facilitating the monitoring of cellular and molecular activities in real-time. Both technologies enhance diagnostic capabilities and therapeutic monitoring.</p></p>
