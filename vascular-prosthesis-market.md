<p><ul><li>Polyester Grafts</li><li>ePTFE</li><li>Polyurethane Grafts</li><li>Biosynthetic Grafts</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vascular prosthesis market comprises various types of grafts used for surgical procedures. Polyester grafts are synthetic materials favored for their strength and durability. ePTFE (expanded polytetrafluoroethylene) grafts are noted for their biocompatibility and resistance to thrombosis. Polyurethane grafts offer flexibility and enhanced performance under physiological conditions. Biosynthetic grafts combine synthetic and biological materials, promoting improved integration with the body's tissues. Each type addresses specific vascular repair needs and patient conditions, influencing clinical choices in vascular surgery.</p></p>
